# El Niño Weather Monitoring Dashboard for California

## Background
El Niño is the warm phase of the El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO), a recurring climate pattern across the tropical Pacific Ocean. During El Niño, sea‑surface temperatures in the central and eastern tropical Pacific become substantially warmer than average, weakening the east–west atmospheric circulation. This alteration in global atmospheric circulation leads to weather changes worldwide, including wetter conditions in California and the southern United States, and impacts on agriculture, fisheries, and the risk of floods or wildfires.
